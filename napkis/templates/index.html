What is Napkis?

One day while we were dickering around in the lab, we found out that it was possbile to make buttons from paper. And it's not even that hard.

So we've implemented a mobile app that takes orders from areas a user touches on a piece of paper. So the next time you go to a resturant, you just have to touch what you want on the menu to order. No expensive touch screens or waiting for busy waiters. After confirming your order, the cooks of the restaurant will be notified on their mobile device and start on the orders immediately.

Why would anyone use this?

We believe restaurants could benefit from changing things up. Right now about 99% of restaurants follow these steps:

  • New people walk in and grab a table
  • They wait for the waiter
  • The waiter has to serve others and eventually ask what the new people want to drink
  • The waiter serves others and grabs the drinks.
  • The waiter then asks new people if they are ready to order
Let's stop there. As you can see, a lot is dependent on the waiter. We want to offer a free service for restaurants to offer a flexible method to enable communication between the chefs and the customers.

All you have to do is touch a menu. No touch screens needed.

By doing this,

  • Waiters get relieved of stress
  • Customers get served faster
  • Restaurants save money

What more could you ask for?

How does it work?

As it turns out, our hands have capacitive charges on them. Always. That means you are always distributing a charge whenever you touch something. Usually it's very small and you'd never notice.

And we found you can move that charge around using something like simple pencil graphite. This means you can easily hook up a small microchip to drawings on paper and check if any charges are being distributed. Or in our case, being touched.

So our application allows restaurants to draw (or print) out any menu and our little, custom made microchip will sync it with out mobile app service.
